\title{Neutron Stars as Gravitationally Confined Macro-Nuclei:\\ A Unified View from Finite Nuclei to the Largest Nuclear Systems}

\author{Md.~Faridul Islam Chowdhury}
\affiliation{Tanfarid Vision Research Institute, Bogura, Bangladesh}
\email{faridul.islam@tanfarid.org}
\date{November 2025}

\begin{abstract}
Above nuclear saturation density, individual atomic nuclei dissolve into a single, homogeneous, $\beta$-equilibrated nuclear medium. We argue that the core of a neutron star is therefore the largest \emph{known} self-bound nuclear system in the Universe—a gravitationally confined macro-nucleus with baryon number $A \sim 10^{57}$--$10^{58}$. This perspective is not metaphorical: the same strong-interaction physics governs ordinary nuclei and neutron-star interiors across 18 orders of magnitude in $A$. Modern chiral effective field theory, ab-initio many-body calculations, and multi-messenger observations (NICER, LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA, pulsar timing) now provide quantitative constraints on the nuclear equation of state from light nuclei to these cosmic-scale nuclear objects, establishing neutron stars as unique laboratories for bulk strong-interaction physics.
\end{abstract}

\section{From Atomic Nuclei to Cosmic-Scale Nuclei}
The conventional description of neutron stars as “collapsed stellar cores” or “degenerate neutron matter” obscures their true structural identity. At densities exceeding nuclear saturation $\rho_0 \approx 0.16$~fm$^{-3}$ ($2.8 \times 10^{17}$~kg~m$^{-3}$), matter undergoes a well-defined sequence of transitions~\cite{Baym1971,Lattimer2016,Horowitz2015}:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Complete pressure ionization of atoms
  \item Electron capture and neutronization ($p + e^- \to n + \nu_e$)
  \item Dissolution of individual nuclei and nuclear-pasta phases into uniform nuclear matter above $\sim 2\rho_0$~\cite{Schneider2014}
  \item Emergence of bulk neutron superfluid and proton superconducting condensates
\end{enumerate}

Beyond this threshold, the distinction between “nucleus” and “inter-nuclear space” vanishes. The stellar interior becomes a single, coherent quantum fluid—a macro-nucleus whose internal structure is governed by the strong nuclear force and whose global stability against collapse is provided by general relativity.

\section{Quantitative Continuity Across 18 Orders of Magnitude}
The energy per baryon $E/A(n_B,\delta)$—with isospin asymmetry $\delta=(n_n-n_p)/n_B$—is a smooth, universal function from stable nuclei ($\delta \approx 0.2$, $n_B \approx \rho_0$) to neutron-star cores ($\delta \gtrsim 0.9$, $n_B \lesssim 5$--$8\rho_0$)~\cite{Epelbaum2009,Machleidt2011,Carlson2015,Drischler2021}.

Chiral effective field theory ($\chi$EFT) and quantum Monte Carlo methods simultaneously describe light-to-medium nuclei and neutron matter up to $\sim 2\rho_0$ with controlled uncertainties~\cite{Lonardoni2018,Tews2018}. Bayesian analyses combining NICER radii, GW170817 tidal deformability, and maximum-mass constraints strongly favor equations of state that adhere to $\chi$EFT predictions up to $\sim 2\rho_0$ and stiffen moderately at higher densities~\cite{Annala2018,Essick2024,Raaijmakers2025}.

\section{Macroscopic Quantum Phenomena as Finite-Size Effects}
Neutron superfluidity and proton superconductivity arise from the same pairing interactions that cause odd–even staggering in finite nuclei. With $A \sim 10^{57}$, these microscopic condensates produce directly observable macroscopic effects:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Quantized vortex lattices and collective unpinning events (pulsar glitches)~\cite{Andersson2012,Montoli2023}
  \item Proton flux tubes sustaining magnetar fields ($B \sim 10^{15}$~G)
  \item Crust–core coupling observable in post-glitch relaxation and cooling curves
\end{itemize}

These are finite-size and rotational analogues of nuclear spectroscopic phenomena, amplified to astronomical scales.

\section{Observational Implications and Future Tests}
The macro-nucleus paradigm is falsifiable and predictive:

\begin{itemize}
  \item NICER, eXTP, and STROBE-X constrain pressure at $1$--$2\rho_0$
  \item Third-generation detectors (Einstein Telescope, Cosmic Explorer) will probe the EOS to $\sim 5$--$8\rho_0$ via tidal deformability
  \item Glitch statistics and neutron-star cooling directly test pairing gaps from nuclear physics
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
Neutron stars are the natural extension of ordinary atomic nuclei to the largest baryon numbers permitted by general relativity and QCD. Recognizing them as gravitationally confined macro-nuclei predicts that neutron-star observables—tidal deformabilities, glitch recovery timescales, and cooling rates—should map directly onto nuclear-theory parameters with minimal phenomenological adjustment, a unification now being quantitatively tested by multi-messenger astronomy.
